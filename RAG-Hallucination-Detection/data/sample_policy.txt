Employee Policy Manual — Version 3.2 (2024)

1. LEAVE POLICY
───────────────
Full-time employees are entitled to 15 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
Part-time employees (working fewer than 30 hours/week) receive 7 days of paid leave.
Unused leave may be carried over to the following year, up to a maximum of 5 days.
Leave requests must be submitted at least 14 days in advance via the HR portal.
Emergency leave (up to 3 days) may be approved by direct managers without advance notice.

2. WORK HOURS
─────────────
Standard office hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday.
All employees are expected to work 40 hours per week.
Flexible work arrangements (flex-time) are available with manager approval.
Overtime is compensated at 1.5× the standard hourly rate for all hours beyond 40/week.
Night shift differential: employees working between 10 PM and 6 AM receive a 20% pay premium.

3. REMOTE WORK POLICY
──────────────────────
Employees may work remotely up to 2 days per week with prior manager approval.
All remote workers must be reachable and responsive during core hours: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM.
A reliable internet connection of at least 10 Mbps is required for remote work.
Remote work privileges may be revoked if performance metrics decline.

4. HEALTH AND WELLNESS BENEFITS
─────────────────────────────────
The company covers 80% of health insurance premiums for all full-time employees.
Dental and vision coverage is included in the standard health plan at no extra cost.
Employees may add dependents (spouse, children) to their health plan; premiums for
  dependents are borne entirely by the employee.
A gym membership reimbursement of up to ₹3,000 per month is available upon submission
  of receipts to the HR department.
Mental health support: employees are entitled to 6 free counselling sessions per year
  through our Employee Assistance Programme (EAP).

5. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
───────────────────────
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: in June and December.
Employees scoring "Exceeds Expectations" are eligible for a merit raise of 8-12%.
Employees scoring "Meets Expectations" are eligible for a 4-7% raise.
Employees scoring "Needs Improvement" will enter a 90-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
Promotion decisions are made annually in January based on cumulative review scores.

6. CODE OF CONDUCT
──────────────────
All employees are expected to act with honesty, integrity, and respect.
Discrimination, harassment, or bullying of any kind will not be tolerated and may
  result in immediate termination.
Confidential company information must not be shared with external parties.
Personal use of company equipment for non-work activities is permitted in moderation.
Violations of this code may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

7. TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT
────────────────────────────
Each employee receives an annual learning budget of ₹25,000 for courses, certifications,
  and conferences.
The company sponsors up to 2 external certifications per employee per year.
Mandatory compliance training must be completed within 30 days of joining.
Internal knowledge-sharing sessions ("Tech Talks") are held every second Friday.

8. GRIEVANCE POLICY
────────────────────
Employees with workplace grievances should first raise the issue with their direct manager.
If unresolved within 7 working days, the grievance may be escalated to the HR department.
All grievances are treated confidentially and investigated within 21 working days.
Retaliation against employees who raise legitimate grievances is strictly prohibited.
